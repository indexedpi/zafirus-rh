# Product

## Register

product

## Users

RRHH operators at Zafirus Technologies. They process employee onboarding cases daily: creating cases, sending candidate forms, reviewing submitted data, consolidating fiscal and payment information, and triggering activation. They work at a desk, typically during business hours, managing several active cases at once. Their primary concern is accuracy and speed; they are not using this tool for pleasure, they are using it to get onboarding done without errors.

## Product Purpose

Replaces PeopleForce as the internal onboarding platform for Zafirus. Centralizes the full lifecycle: case creation, candidate data collection via a dedicated form, RRHH review, task tracking, email communication, and data consolidation (CUIT, CBU). Success looks like zero missed onboarding steps, full data traceability, and no back-and-forth over missing information.

## Brand Personality

Profesional, Claro, Eficiente. The interface is serious without being cold. It respects the operator's time: no decorative noise, no gamification, no unnecessary animation. It earns trust through precision and predictability.

## Anti-references

- **SAP / Oracle HCM**: dense grids, legacy enterprise UX, information buried in tabs inside tabs, no visual hierarchy.
- **Monday.com / Asana (colorful mode)**: saturated palettes, startup-juvenile feel, gamification affordances that infantilize a professional task.
- **ADP / Workday generic**: flat, form-heavy, zero visual identity, feels like filling out a government document.
- **Notion minimalist extreme**: so stripped it looks unfinished; absence of affordance masquerading as design.

## Design Principles

1. **Operability over decoration.** Every visual element must serve a workflow step. Nothing exists to fill space or signal effort.
2. **Data clarity first.** Dense operational data (employee records, candidate fields, audit logs) must be scannable at a glance. Hierarchy is a functional tool, not an aesthetic one.
3. **Earned color.** Color communicates state and priority (success, warning, pending, active). It is not used for brand enthusiasm or visual interest.
4. **Predictable precision.** Operators develop muscle memory. Consistent placement, consistent interaction patterns, consistent feedback. Surprises are bugs, not features.
5. **Human enough to trust.** Serious enough for real employee data. Warm enough that using it for 6 hours straight doesn't feel like an audit.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Minimum contrast ratio 4.5:1 for body text, 3:1 for large text and UI components. Full keyboard navigation. Focus indicators visible. No information conveyed by color alone.
